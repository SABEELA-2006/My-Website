FRUITS AND VEGETABLES

Fruits and vegetables are essential plant-based foods providing vital vitamins, minerals, fiber, and antioxidants, crucial for preventing chronic diseases like heart disease, diabetes, and some cancers, with variety in color and type being key for diverse nutrients, and generally recommended daily intake being around 5 portions for good health.

FRUITS

Fruits are the sweet, fleshy, ripened ovaries of flowering plants, containing seeds, crucial for a healthy diet with vitamins and fiber, and come in varieties like berries, citrus, tropical, and stone fruits, including common ones like apples, bananas, oranges, mangoes, and also botanically considered fruits like tomatoes and avocados.

VEGETABLES

Vegetables are edible parts of plants that are consumed by humans or other animals as food. This original meaning is still commonly used, and is applied to plants collectively to refer to all edible plant matter, including flowers, fruits, stems, leaves, roots, and seeds.

EXAMPLES OF FRUITS

Examples of fruits include common sweet ones like apples, bananas, oranges, grapes, strawberries, and mangoes, but botanically, fruits also encompass things like tomatoes, cucumbers, avocados, nuts, and corn kernels, as they all develop from a plant's flower and contain seeds. Types range from berries (blueberries), drupes (peaches, cherries), pomes (pears).
